
## 3.1. X-ray Spectrum 
- The coronal X-ray emission can be simply characterized by a power law with a photon index ($Γ$) and cut-off energy ($E_C$), such that the flux:
$$F (E) ∝ E^{−Γ}e^{−E/E_C}$$
- $Γ$: the spectral slope, which is related to the temperature of the Comptonizing electrons ($kT_e$) and the optical depth ($τ$) of the X-ray emitting plasma (Rybicki & Lightman 1979) 
- $E_C$: the high energy cut-off 
- 보편적인 X-ray 광도 범위는 $L_{0.3−100}$ keV를 가진다.

- The coronal X-ray spectrum is a power-law in the energy range ∼ 0.3 − 100 keV (Vaiana & Rosner 1978; Haardt & Maraschi 1993; Merloni et al. 2003), and contributes to around 5 − 10% of AGN bolometric luminosity (Elvis et al. 1994; Marconi et al. 2004; Vasudevan & Fabian 2007; Fabian et al. 2017).
